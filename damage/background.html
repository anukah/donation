BACKGROUND AND SITUATION

Understanding the impact of the Ditwah Cyclone on Peradeniya

The Disaster

The Ditwah Cyclone

The Ditwah Cyclone brought unprecedented rainfall to large parts of Sri Lanka, triggering widespread floods and landslides. River systems overflowed, hillsides became unstable, and critical infrastructure across the country was severely damaged.

While many districts were affected, the scale and intensity of the disaster placed extraordinary pressure on regions located within major river basins and steep terrain — including the University of Peradeniya.

Vulnerability

Why Peradeniya Was Severely Affected

The University of Peradeniya is located within the Mahaweli River basin and surrounded by hilly and sloped terrain. This geographic sensitivity significantly increased exposure to flooding, erosion, and slope failures during extreme rainfall.

Observed Impacts

Severe flooding across low-lying areas
Slope failures and soil erosion
Landslides affecting buildings and access roads
Damage to utilities and internal infrastructure
Context

About the University of Peradeniya

The University of Peradeniya is Sri Lanka’s oldest and largest comprehensive university, serving tens of thousands of students across multiple faculties. It plays a critical national role in education, research, healthcare, agriculture, and professional training.

“The total estimated damage to the University exceeds LKR 4 billion.”

Consequences

Immediate Impacts

Academic Disruption

Teaching, laboratory work, examinations, and research activities were suspended due to unsafe conditions and damaged facilities.

Safety Risks

Flooded buildings, unstable slopes, and damaged infrastructure posed serious safety risks to students, staff, and residents.

Student Displacement

More than 1,300 students were displaced from hostels and residences, requiring immediate relocation and support.
