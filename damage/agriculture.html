Faculty of Agriculture

Severe damage to one of the University of Peradeniya’s key academic faculties, disrupting teaching, learning, and administrative operations.

Overview

A Nationally Critical Faculty

The Faculty of Agriculture plays a central role in Sri Lanka’s food security, agricultural innovation, and rural development. The floods caused extensive damage to laboratories, research facilities, and essential equipment, severely disrupting teaching, experimentation, and field-based operations.

Disruption to this faculty directly threatens agricultural research, farmer support systems, and national food resilience.

Impacted Areas

Departments Affected

Department of Animal Science

Flood damage to laboratories, farm support systems, and teaching facilities disrupted animal production, nutrition studies, and applied research critical to livestock development.

Soil Science Laboratories

Flooding compromised soil testing instruments, sample storage units, and laboratory safety systems, delaying research and field-based agricultural assessments.

Agricultural Engineering

Damage to workshops, machinery, and mechanical facilities restricted practical training, equipment maintenance, and engineering support essential for modern agricultural operations.

Related Projects

Proposed Interventions

L3 – Laboratory Restoration

Restoration of damaged laboratories to reinstate teaching, research activities, and essential academic operations.

Estimated Cost: Rs. 100 Million

L4 – Equipment Replacement

Replacement of flood-damaged laboratory instruments, machinery, and teaching equipment critical for faculty operations.

E3 – Electrical Infrastructure

Repair and upgrading of electrical systems to ensure safety, reliability, and uninterrupted laboratory functionality.

C2 / C-D / J – Slope Stabilization Works

Engineering interventions to stabilize affected slopes, mitigate future risks, and protect faculty infrastructure.
